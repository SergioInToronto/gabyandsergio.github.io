Things to do

We're so excited to share with you some of our favourite things to do in the area! Please see the FAQs for more tips/recommendations.

Walk…Everywhere!
Exploring on foot is the best way to get a feel for the city, and most areas of PV are reachable by walking.
This is a great way to see what the vendors are selling, to people watch, and get a lay of the land. You never know what you'll stumble upon when discovering the city on foot — just make sure to wear good shoes as the sidewalks and roads are uneven.
Wander The Old Town
Experience the charm of Puerto Vallarta's old town by taking a peaceful walk up the hill from the Church of Our Lady of Guadalupe. Enjoy the tranquility of the "upper" roads, away from the crowds, and emerge near the River when you're ready to head back.
Walking Tour
The free walking tour around Puerto Vallarta is the perfect activity to explore the city! You'll be led by a certified guide through the historic downtown area.
View
Walk The Malecón
Enjoy a leisurely walk without the hassle of busy traffic on Puerto Vallarta's 1.5 km long Malecón oceanfront promenade. Take in the beautiful ocean views and savor a relaxing stroll.
View
Day of the Death - November 2nd
Celebrate the Mexican holiday of Dia de los Muertos, which honors those who have passed away, during your visit to Puerto Vallarta. Unlike Halloween, this holiday is a joyful occasion that welcomes the return of loved ones.
Immerse yourself in the local culture and experience the Puerto Vallarta Day of the Dead celebrations, including street parties and altars throughout the city. Don't miss the giant catrina statues set up along the Malecón boardwalk in the days leading up to the festivities.
See Rhythms Of The Night
Don't miss the Rhythms of The Night show and dinner experience in Puerto Vallarta. Start the evening with a sunset cruise across Banderas Bay to Las Caletas, a stunning private cove.
Enjoy a cultural performance featuring live music, dancing, contortionists, fire twirlers, and more. Afterward, savor a delicious buffet dinner before returning to Puerto Vallarta by boat.
View
Lookout Cerro de La Cruz
For an amazing view, you can hike up to the Mirador Cerro de La Cruz. It takes about 30 minutes and is a bit strenuous, but the view is spectacular. Bring lots of water and wear good shoes.
View
Visit Marietas Island
A limited number of people per day are allowed to visit this gorgeous site — make sure you book online ahead of time.
Located about a 1-hour boat ride from the Puerto Vallarta marina, you'll find the Marietas hidden beach. Not only is the snorkelling and the beach itself gorgeous, but on the boat ride out there you may spot dolphins, manta rays, or whales.
View
Check Out The Markets
At the many markets in Puerto Vallarta you'll find food, clothing, souvenirs, and more. The Colonia Emiliano Zapata market is located in Zona Romántica and is open everyday.
View
Go Scuba Diving
You can expect to see turtles, rays, eels, and more. Always do your own independent research when choosing a scuba diving company. Check reviews, safety, and certifications, and even stop by the dive center to get a feel for the place before committing.
Church Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe
Puerto Vallarta's Our Lady of Guadalupe Parish (Parroquia de Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe) is a gorgeous cathedral that dominates the skyline of the city – truly postcard perfect It was built back in 1903 and has been an important part of the cultural and spiritual life of the city.
View
Botanical Gardens
The Sierra Madre mountain range has a generous diversity and tropical forests that make Puerto Vallarta an enchanting place way beyond its beaches.
View
Day trip to Tequila
Did you know that the spirit “tequila” was actually named after the town where it was first created? That's right, there is a town in Mexico called “Tequila” (with a capital -T) that is the birthplace of tequila (the alcoholic drink!)
And what's better, the town of Tequila is located only about a 3.5-hour drive from Puerto Vallarta!
